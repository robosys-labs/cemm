# CEMM v3.4 — AGENTS.md Reliability Delta

Merge these laws into the governing `AGENTS.md`.

## Grounded-understanding law

Schema reference resolution is not understanding.

A schema revision may become operationally active only when its schema-family definition is structurally complete, its required dependencies terminate in executable foundations or grounded active schemas, unsupported circularity is absent, and required competence cases pass.

## Opaque-concept law

CEMM may create references to unknown concepts and may preserve attributed propositions about them.

An opaque or partial schema may be quoted, remembered, queried as an assertion, searched, contrasted provisionally, or targeted for learning. It may not authorize inheritance, effects, state mutation, strong definition synthesis, or an `understands` claim.

## Primitive-foundation law

Domain concepts such as person, animal, leader, president, engineer, organization, or software agent are not kernel primitives.

Kernel foundations are formal semantic representations, value types, and a small audited set of executable relation/operation schemas with implemented truth, query, identity, and inference behavior.

Audited boot concepts are ordinary active schema records with provenance and grounding specifications.

## Definition-shape law

Learning validation is schema-family-specific.

Entity kinds, role-like concepts, predicates, states, and events have different required definition fields and competency cases. A generic count of claims, edges, or examples cannot establish understanding.

## Semantic-sufficiency law

Definition closure and epistemic confidence are separate.

A single complete compositional definition may establish semantic sufficiency. Multiple circular, redundant, or purely taxonomic assertions may establish none. Source support and counterevidence control confidence and promotion scope, not structural meaning.

## Inheritance law

A child schema may not inherit executable roles, constraints, effects, or query behavior from an opaque or partial parent.

A taxonomic statement involving unresolved schemas remains attributed/deferred evidence until exact relation type and parent grounding are resolved. A specialized child also requires a differentiating condition unless the teaching is explicitly a synonym/alias claim.

## Learning-outcome law

The system may say it learned or understood a concept only when the exact executable schema revision activated, the ordinary resolver used it, replay succeeded, and required competence checks passed.

Remembering a statement is not learning the concept expressed by the statement.

## Competence-independence law

Activation may not be self-certified. Competence cases derived only from the teaching evidence they validate check well-formedness, not discrimination. A single source may not supply both a definition and its only discriminating competence cases beyond session/user scope.

## Jointly-anchored-cycle law

Mutually defined schemas may activate together only when their dependency component has external grounded anchors, non-redundant constitutive contributions, and a joint discriminating competence suite. Unanchored cycles never activate regardless of assertion count.

## Effect-authorization law

Structural executability never grants effect authority. Schemas with precondition/effect semantics require a separate effect-authorization decision considering source trust, scope, permission, and risk class before projecting any state or action effect.

## Assessment-invalidation law

A grounding assessment is valid only for its exact revision and dependency closure fingerprint. Dependency supersession, quarantine, or retirement invalidates dependent assessments and downgrades usability through a traceable cascade. No component may keep consuming a stale executable status.

## Probe-budget law

Definition regress is bounded. Learning episodes carry explicit probe depth and open-gap budgets; an exhausted budget leaves typed gaps and an honest partial status — never unbounded questioning and never fabricated grounding.

## Defeasibility law

Constitutive and differentiating patterns carry explicit strength (strict, defeasible, typical). Exceptions to defeasible patterns are recorded as exceptions, not contradictions, and do not defeat closure. Patterns induced from instance evidence enter at defeasible strength at most and never activate a schema alone.

## Sense-individuation law

Lexemes and schemas are different identity spaces. Structurally incompatible teaching defaults to a candidate new sense, not a contradiction, unless explicitly marked as a correction. Schema refs unify only through an explicit, journaled, reversible same-identity assessment. Provisional refs are durable and re-resolvable so deferred evidence accumulates instead of fragmenting.

## Derived-evidence law

Every proposition carries derivation provenance. An inferred proposition may not increase support, confidence, or competence standing for any schema revision in its own derivation ancestry. Inference outputs are working knowledge, never laundered self-support.

## Proposition–revision-binding law

A committed proposition binds to the schema revisions under which it was interpreted at assertion time. Later schema revisions reinterpret through explicit journaled replay; they never silently rewrite stored meaning.

## Self-report law

Epistemic verbs (remembers, has-evidence-for, understands, uncertain-about) are executable foundational relations over queryable records. Every clause of a self-report binds to a derivable epistemic record; an unbacked self-claim is a realization error. When the relation cannot be derived, the honest output is uncertainty.

## Expressiveness-blocker law

A definition requiring negation, cardinality, quantification, modality, or temporal scope that the pattern language cannot express produces a concrete expressiveness blocker. Dropping the construct to force closure is fabrication and is forbidden.
